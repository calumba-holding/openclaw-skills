# Getting Around Portugal

## Between Cities

### Trains (CP)
**Website:** cp.pt
**Types:**
- **Alfa Pendular** — Fast (Lisbon-Porto 3h), comfortable, €35
- **Intercidades** — Slower, cheaper, €25
- **Regional** — Local, very cheap, scenic (Douro!)

**Key routes:**
| Route | Time | Price | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|-------|
| Lisbon → Porto | 3h | €35 | Alfa Pendular, book ahead |
| Lisbon → Faro | 2h30 | €25 | Good connection |
| Porto → Braga | 1h | €3.50 | Frequent |
| Porto → Guimarães | 1h15 | €3.50 | |
| Porto → Pinhão (Douro) | 3h | €14 | Scenic, beautiful! |

**Tips:**
- Book Alfa Pendular online for best prices
- Regional trains: buy at station (no reservations)
- First class rarely worth it on short routes

### Buses (Rede Expressos)
**Website:** rede-expressos.pt
**Why:** Often cheaper than trains, reach more places
**Comfort:** Good, modern coaches

**Key routes:**
| Route | Time | Price |
|-------|------|-------|
| Lisbon → Porto | 3h30 | €20 |
| Lisbon → Algarve | 3-4h | €20 |
| Lisbon → Évora | 1h30 | €13 |

**Other companies:**
- FlixBus — Some routes
- EVA — Algarve regional
- Rodonorte — North

### Flights
**Domestic airlines:** TAP, Ryanair, easyJet

**When worthwhile:**
- Lisbon ↔ Porto (1h, sometimes cheaper than train)
- Mainland ↔ Madeira
- Mainland ↔ Azores
- Between Azores islands (SATA)

**Book:** Directly or via Skyscanner

## Car Rental

### When You Need a Car
- Alentejo (essential)
- Algarve beyond main towns
- Douro Valley (unless doing train + tours)
- Madeira
- Azores
- Centro region
- Rota Vicentina access

### When You Don't
- Lisbon (nightmare to drive, park)
- Porto (walkable, good metro)
- Train routes only

### Rental Companies
- International: Hertz, Avis, Europcar, Sixt
- Good value: Auto Jardim, Guerin
- Book via: Discover Cars, Rentalcars for comparison

### Costs
- Economy car: €25-40/day
- SUV: €50-80/day
- August: 2x prices, book early
- Tip: Pick up at airport (cheaper than city)

### Tolls
⚠️ **Portugal has electronic tolls (no cash booths)**

**Options:**
1. **Via Verde Visitors** — App + device, best for long stays
2. **Toll card from rental** — €1-2/day extra, easiest
3. **Post-pay** — Register plates at post office within 5 days
4. **Pay at CTT** — Post office, within 5 days of using toll

**Key toll roads:**
- A1: Lisbon-Porto (€22 full length)
- A22: Algarve coast (€5-10)
- A2: Lisbon-Algarve (€18)

**Alternative:** N roads (no tolls, slower, scenic)

### Driving Tips
- Right side (like rest of Europe)
- Speed limits: 50 urban, 90 rural, 120 motorway
- Radar cameras common
- Fuel: Full-serve disappearing, use self-serve (mais barato)
- Parking: Blue zones paid, sometimes only Mon-Sat
- Lisbon: Avoid. Seriously.

## Within Lisbon

### Metro
**Lines:** 4 (Blue, Yellow, Green, Red)
**Hours:** 6:30-1:00
**Price:** €1.65/trip (need Viva Viagem card €0.50)
**Tip:** Google Maps has good Metro integration

### Trams
**Historic:** 28, 12E, others
**Warning:** Tram 28 = pickpocket central, tourist overcrowded
**Alternative:** Walk Alfama, take 12E instead

### Buses
**Operator:** Carris
**Useful for:** Where metro doesn't go
**Price:** Same €1.65 with card

### Elevators & Funiculars
- **Santa Justa Elevator:** €5 (skip, walk around)
- **Bica Funicular:** €4 (Instagram famous)
- **Glória Funicular:** €4 (up to Bairro Alto)

**Tip:** Free if you have 24h transport pass

### Uber/Bolt
- Everywhere
- €5-15 most trips
- Often best option late night

### 24h Pass
**Carris/Metro pass:** €6.80/day
Worth it if: 4+ metro/bus trips

## Within Porto

### Metro
**Lines:** 6 (A-F)
**Airport:** Line E (violet) — essential
**Hours:** 6:00-1:00
**Price:** €1.30-2.60 depending on zones
**Tip:** Buy Andante card (€0.60)

### Buses (STCP)
**Use for:** Where metro doesn't go
**Same Andante card works**

### Historic Tram
**Line 1:** Infante to Foz (scenic, along river)
**Lines 18, 22:** Tourist routes
**Tip:** Not practical, but Line 1 is nice once

### Funicular dos Guindais
Connects Ribeira to Batalha. Scenic, useful.

### Walking
Best way. Porto is walkable but hilly.

## Within Algarve

### Without Car
**Train:** Faro-Lagos line, connects coast
**Bus:** EVA for local routes
**Uber:** Works in main towns
**Reality:** Limited without car

### With Car (Recommended)
- Easy driving
- Parking available
- Beaches accessible
- Worth it

### Airport (Faro)
- Connections to Lagos, Tavira by bus
- Uber/taxi €20-40 to towns
- Rent car here if needed

## Islands

### Madeira
**Buses:** Limited, tourist schedules
**Car:** Recommended for exploring
**Taxi:** Expensive
**Tours:** Easy option for non-drivers

**Tip:** Rent car for at least 2-3 days. Roads are winding but good.

### Azores (São Miguel)
**Buses:** Very limited
**Car:** Essential
**Taxi:** Expensive for touring

**Tip:** Rent car entire stay. Island small but spread out.

### Between Azores Islands
**Flights:** SATA Air Açores
**Ferries:** Limited, seasonal (check Atlânticoline)
**Tip:** Flights more reliable than ferries

## Getting to/from Airports

### Lisbon (LIS)
- **Metro:** Red line to Alameda, change. 30 min, €1.65
- **Aerobus:** €4, every 20 min
- **Uber/Bolt:** €10-15
- **Taxi:** €15-20 (fixed rate if metered)

### Porto (OPO)
- **Metro:** Line E, 30 min, €2.60
- **Uber/Bolt:** €15-20
- **Taxi:** €20-25

### Faro (FAO)
- **Bus:** To Faro center, infrequent
- **Uber/Bolt:** Limited availability
- **Taxi:** €15-20 to Faro center
- **Transfer:** Often best if going to Lagos/Tavira
- **Rent car:** Pick up at airport

### Funchal (Madeira)
- **Bus:** Aerobus, €5
- **Taxi:** €25-30 to Funchal
- **Transfer:** Book with hotel

### Ponta Delgada (Azores)
- **Taxi:** €10-15 to center
- **Bus:** Limited
- **Rent car:** Pick up at airport
